**Your Exposure:**

- Freelancer/contractor unpaid invoice

**Without LLC:** You're personally liable

**With LLC:** Business liability only

#### 5. **Employment Issues** 🟢 LOW RISK (for now)

## 📊 PART 3: TAX IMPLICATIONS

### Single-Member LLC (Default: Disregarded Entity)

**How It Works:**
- LLC is "disregarded" for tax purposes
- You file taxes on Schedule C (sole proprietor)
- No separate LLC tax return needed (simple!)
- All profit flows through to your personal 1040

**Your Tax Situation:**

```
Your 9-to-5 W-2 Income: $XXX,XXX
+ WorthApply LLC Profit: $XX,XXX (Schedule C)
= Total Income: $XXX,XXX
```

**Tax Rate:**
- Same as now (marginal tax bracket)
- Plus self-employment tax (15.3%) on LLC profit

**Example:**
- 9-to-5 income: $120,000 (24% bracket)
- WorthApply profit: $50,000
- Additional tax on WorthApply:
  - Federal income tax: $12,000 (24%)
  - Self-employment tax: $7,650 (15.3%)
  - **Total:** ~$19,650
  - **Effective rate:** 39.3%

### S-Corp Election (Optional, Later)

**When to Consider:**
- When profit > $60,000/year
- Saves on self-employment tax

**How It Works:**
- Pay yourself "reasonable salary" (W-2)
- Remaining profit taken as "distributions" (no SE tax)

**Example at $100k profit:**

**As LLC (disregarded):**
- Self-employment tax: $15,300 (15.3%)

**As S-Corp:**
- Salary: $60,000 → SE tax: $9,180
- Distributions: $40,000 → SE tax: $0
- **Savings:** $6,120/year

**Cost of S-Corp:**
- Payroll processing: $600-1,200/year
- Accountant: $1,000-2,500/year
- **Net savings:** $2,820-4,520/year

**Recommendation:**
- Start as single-member LLC (simple)
- Elect S-Corp when profit > $60k/year
- File Form 2553 with IRS (can do anytime)

## 🌎 PART 6: WHICH STATE TO FORM IN?

### Your Residence State (RECOMMENDED)

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Only file in one state (simplest)
- ✅ No need to register as "foreign LLC"
- ✅ Can use your home address
- ✅ Lowest total cost

**Disadvantages:**
- ❌ State-dependent fees (CA expensive)
- ❌ State-dependent taxes

**Recommendation:** Unless you're in California (high fees), form in your home state.

### Delaware (Popular for VC-backed startups)

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Business-friendly laws
- ✅ Court of Chancery (business court)
- ✅ Strong legal precedent

**Disadvantages:**
- ❌ Must also register in your home state (double filing)
- ❌ More expensive ($90 DE + $300/year + home state fees)
- ❌ Need registered agent in Delaware

**Recommendation:** Only if raising VC funding (not needed for bootstrapped SaaS)

### Wyoming / Nevada (Low-Cost Options)

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Low fees ($100 WY, $425 NV)
- ✅ Strong privacy protections
- ✅ No state income tax

**Disadvantages:**
- ❌ Must also register in your home state
- ❌ May not save money overall

**Recommendation:** Not worth the complexity for most side businesses

### Bottom Line:

**Form in your home state** unless you have a specific reason not to.

## 📝 PART 7: MAINTAINING LLC COMPLIANCE

### Annual Requirements (2-3 hours/year):

#### 1. **Annual Report** (Most States)
- **When:** Usually on anniversary of formation
- **What:** Update business address, members, registered agent
- **Cost:** $0-500 (varies by state)
- **Penalty for missing:** Dissolution of LLC, personal liability

#### 2. **Taxes** (Time: 2-10 hours/year)

**Simple LLC (Disregarded Entity):**
- File Schedule C with personal 1040
- Quarterly estimated taxes if profit > $1,000
- Self-employment tax (Schedule SE)

**Recommended:**
- Use accounting software (QuickBooks, Wave)
- Hire CPA for first year ($300-800)
- DIY after you understand it

#### 3. **Separate Finances** (CRITICAL!)

**Do This:**
- ✅ Separate business bank account (never mix!)
- ✅ Business credit card (optional but good)
- ✅ Pay yourself distributions (not salary unless S-Corp)
- ✅ Keep receipts for all business expenses
- ✅ Track income and expenses monthly

**Don't Do This:**
- ❌ Never pay personal expenses from business account
- ❌ Never pay business expenses from personal account
- ❌ Never "borrow" money between accounts without documenting

**Why:** "Piercing the corporate veil" - if you mix finances, courts can ignore LLC protection.

#### 4. **Operating Agreement** (One-Time, 30 minutes)

**What:** Internal document outlining how LLC operates

**Why:** Proves you treat LLC as separate entity

**What to Include:**
- Ownership % (100% you)
- Management structure (you as manager)
- Capital contributions (initial investment)
- Profit/loss allocation (100% to you)
- Dissolution procedures
- Buy-sell provisions (if adding partners later)

**Templates:** Free online, or LegalZoom ($100-200)

#### 5. **Hold Annual Meeting** (Yes, Even Alone!)

**What:** Meeting to discuss business, make decisions

**Why:** Shows you treat LLC as separate entity

**How (for single-member):**
- Write meeting minutes (1 page)
- Document decisions (new products, hiring, etc.)
- Keep in LLC records
- Date and sign

**Time:** 30 minutes/year

**Example Agenda:**
- Review financial performance
- Discuss upcoming goals
- Vote on any major decisions
- Document in minutes

## ⚖️ PART 8: ALTERNATIVES TO LLC

### Option 1: Sole Proprietorship (Current Status)

**What It Is:**
- Default structure (no filing needed)
- You = the business
- No legal separation

**Advantages:**
- ✅ No formation cost
- ✅ No annual fees
- ✅ Simple taxes (Schedule C)
- ✅ No compliance requirements

**Disadvantages:**
- ❌ Unlimited personal liability
- ❌ Your house, savings, 401k at risk
- ❌ Less professional credibility
- ❌ Harder to raise funding
- ❌ No tax flexibility

**When to Use:**
- Very low-risk business (consulting, freelance)
- Pre-revenue testing (MVP phase)
- No customer data or contracts

**For WorthApply:** ❌ NOT RECOMMENDED (too much liability risk)

### Option 2: Corporation (C-Corp or S-Corp)

**What It Is:**
- Separate legal entity (like LLC)
- More complex structure
- Board of directors, officers, shareholders

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Liability protection (like LLC)
- ✅ Easier to raise VC funding (C-Corp)
- ✅ Stock options for employees
- ✅ Tax benefits (C-Corp for growth, S-Corp for profit)

**Disadvantages:**
- ❌ More expensive ($500-2,000 formation)
- ❌ More complex compliance (board meetings, bylaws)
- ❌ Double taxation (C-Corp)
- ❌ More paperwork (annual reports, minutes)

**When to Use:**
- Raising venture capital (C-Corp)
- High profit, want to save on taxes (S-Corp)
- Planning to go public someday

**For WorthApply:** ❌ Overkill for now (can convert LLC later)

### Option 3: DBA (Doing Business As)

**What It Is:**
- "Fictitious name" registration
- Still a sole proprietor
- Just using a business name

**Advantages:**
- ✅ Cheap ($10-100)
- ✅ Can open business bank account
- ✅ Looks more professional

**Disadvantages:**
- ❌ No liability protection
- ❌ Still personally liable

**For WorthApply:** ❌ Doesn't solve liability problem

### Bottom Line:

**For WorthApply, the best choice is Single-Member LLC.**
