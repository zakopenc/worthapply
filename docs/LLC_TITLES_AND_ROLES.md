## 📋 LLC STRUCTURE EXPLAINED

### Two Key Roles in an LLC:

#### 1. **Member** = Owner (You)
- Technical term for LLC owner
- Owns membership units (like stock in corporation)
- You: 100% member (single-member LLC)
- Legal documents use this term

#### 2. **Manager** = Decision Maker
- Person who runs day-to-day operations
- Signs contracts, makes business decisions
- Two options:
  - **Member-managed:** Members run the LLC (you)
  - **Manager-managed:** Hire outside manager (not needed)

**For You:** Member-managed LLC (you're both owner and manager)

## 🏢 OFFICIAL LLC ROLES

### Legal Terms (for LLC documents):

| Role | What It Means | For You |
|------|---------------|---------|
| **Member** | Owner of the LLC | Yes (100% owner) |
| **Managing Member** | Owner who manages operations | Yes (you manage it) |
| **Manager** | Non-owner who manages | No (not needed) |
| **Authorized Signatory** | Can sign contracts on behalf of LLC | Yes (you) |

**In Operating Agreement:** You are "Managing Member" (technical term)

**In Articles of Organization:** May list you as "Organizer" or "Member"

## ⚖️ LEGAL CONSIDERATIONS

### Can You Call Yourself "CEO" of an LLC?

**Answer:** ✅ Yes, absolutely!

**Why:**
- LLC can use any titles
- No legal requirement for specific titles
- CEO is common for LLC-structured startups
- Courts recognize CEO as "authorized agent"

**Legal Authority:**
- "Managing Member" gives you legal authority
- "CEO" is business title you choose
- Both are valid
- Use "Managing Member" on legal docs, "CEO" everywhere else

### Must You Be "Managing Member"?

**For Single-Member LLC:** Yes, that's your legal role.

**But:** You can call yourself anything in business contexts.

**Example:**
- Operating Agreement: "Zak Boch, Managing Member"
- Business card: "Zak Boch, Founder & CEO"
- Both are correct in their contexts
