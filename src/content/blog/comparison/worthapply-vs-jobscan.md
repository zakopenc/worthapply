## Accuracy & Effectiveness

### Does Job Fit Analysis Actually Work?

The underlying logic is simple and well-established: the further you are from a role's must-have requirements, the lower your callback rate — no matter how well the resume is tailored. Recruiters and hiring managers filter on fit signals first (years of experience, required skills, domain, seniority), then on presentation.

Fit-first analysis applies that filter *before* you invest the 15–25 minutes of manual tailoring per application. The goal isn't to promise a specific multiplier on your interview rate — it's to stop you from spending hours on applications that were never going to convert, and to redirect that time toward the 10–15 roles where you actually have a shot.

The algorithm isn't perfect (no AI is). But it surfaces evidence gaps you can either close (by highlighting existing experience you forgot to mention) or accept (by walking away from a role that needs three years of experience you don't have).

### Does Jobscan's ATS Optimization Work?

Jobscan claims 2-3x more interviews with optimized resumes. Third-party reviews confirm this for applicants who:
1. Actually implement the suggestions (many don't)
2. Apply to ATS-heavy companies (small companies often don't use ATS)
3. Are otherwise qualified for the role

**Both tools are effective, but for different problems.**
